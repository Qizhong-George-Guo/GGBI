# The Green–Grey–Blue–Intelligent (GGBI) Framework  
### Integrated Infrastructure & Systems for Resilient Water and Urban Environments

The **GGBI Framework** provides a system-scale lens for understanding and integrating the four foundational domains shaping water and urban environments:

- **Green infrastructure**  
- **Grey infrastructure**  
- **Blue infrastructure**  
- **Intelligent infrastructure & systems**

GGBI connects natural processes, engineered systems, hydrologic and hydraulic functions, sensing networks, modeling tools, and AI-enhanced decision support. It offers a unified structure for designing, managing, and adapting water and urban systems under increasing complexity, uncertainty, and climate-driven change.

---

## What is GGBI?

**The Green–Grey–Blue–Intelligent (GGBI) Framework** is an integrated approach that synthesizes ecological solutions, engineered structures, hydrologic and coastal systems, and intelligent technologies into a coherent, cross-disciplinary model.

GGBI emphasizes:

- **Synergy:** Infrastructure elements do not operate in isolation  
- **Scale:** From parcels and neighborhoods to watersheds, estuaries, and coasts  
- **Data and intelligence:** Monitoring, modeling, forecasting, analytics, and AI  
- **Adaptation:** Responsive design under climate variability and extremes  
- **Co-benefits:** Environmental, social, economic, and resilience goals  
- **Integration:** Joining nature-based, structural, hydrologic, and computational domains  

---

## The Four Infrastructure Domains

### **1. Green Infrastructure**
Nature-based and ecological systems that enhance infiltration, retention, cooling, habitat, and environmental co-benefits.  
Examples include bioretention, vegetated systems, green roofs, wetlands, floodplains, and landscape-scale ecological corridors.

### **2. Grey Infrastructure**
Engineered and structural systems providing conveyance, storage, protection, and mechanical control.  
Examples include stormwater networks, levees, dams, seawalls, tunnels, culverts, detention basins, and pumping systems.

### **3. Blue Infrastructure**
Hydrologic and hydraulic systems including rivers, lakes, estuaries, bays, and coastal waters.  
Blue infrastructure shapes water transport, mixing, sediment processes, tides, storm surge, and fluvial–estuarine–coastal interactions.

### **4. Intelligent Infrastructure & Systems**
Sensing networks, modeling platforms, data analytics, forecasting systems, and AI-enabled tools that support adaptive management, monitoring, and decision-making.

Intelligence integrates:

- environmental sensing and data streams  
- hydrologic, hydraulic, estuarine, and coastal modeling  
- scenario analysis and optimization  
- early warning and emergency response  
- AI-assisted synthesis, communication, and education  

---

## Why “Intelligent”?

While **smart infrastructure** often focuses on sensors and IoT devices, **intelligent infrastructure & systems** encompasses:

- sensing  
- analytics  
- modeling  
- data fusion  
- optimization  
- decision support  
- artificial intelligence  
- and domain knowledge integration

This broader definition aligns with emerging practice and reflects the analytical, modeling, and AI-supported approaches increasingly central to resilient water and urban systems.

---

## Why GGBI?

GGBI supports planning, design, operation, and policy development across a wide range of challenges:

- Urban stormwater and pluvial flooding  
- Watershed and river-basin management  
- Estuarine and coastal processes  
- Green stormwater infrastructure and nature-based solutions  
- Sediment transport and water quality  
- Climate change and extreme events  
- Cross-sector resilience and environmental equity  

The framework is designed to bridge the full continuum of water systems — from rainfall to runoff, rivers to estuaries, and coasts to ocean — while incorporating the power of modern data, computation, and intelligence.

---

## A Systems View

GGBI underscores that resilient, sustainable, and equitable water systems require:

- understanding interactions among infrastructure types  
- integrating natural and built environments  
- connecting upland, riverine, estuarine, and coastal processes  
- blending physical, ecological, and computational knowledge  
- applying advanced tools for forecasting and decision-making  

This systems perspective guides research, education, infrastructure planning, and interdisciplinary collaboration.

---

## A Living and Evolving Framework

The GGBI Framework is continuously evolving as new insights, technologies, and climate challenges emerge. This website serves as a public resource for:

- conceptual models  
- research synthesis  
- academic and professional materials  
- emerging AI-based tools  
- examples and applications across scales  

GGBI is intended to support researchers, practitioners, students, and communities working toward **integrated, adaptive, and intelligent water systems**.

- 绿色、灰色、蓝色与智能系统的协同与互补

- 数据驱动与智能化的分析与决策  
- 面对气候变化与极端事件的适应与演化  
- 跨学科的系统观与整体解决方案  

GGBI 是一个持续发展的开放性框架，用于支持科研、教学、政策制定、规划设计以及公众交流。
